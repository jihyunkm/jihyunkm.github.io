Responses

  • What is the goal of the website? Who is it intended for? How does the design accomplish this? Write 2-3 sentences answering these questions. (Use the 'p' tag again!)

    The goal of YouTube is to act as a platform where creators can upload their own videos and for anyone to be able to access and enjoy the videos. Their intended audience is “everyone,” as can be seen from their mission statement—”giv[ing] everyone a voice and show[ing] them the world.” The design accomplishes this by being easily navigable and accessible; the site utilizes many common UI patterns such as a search bar, (video) cards, sidebar menu, etc.

  • Write 2-3 sentences about what problems your redesign addressed, and how it solved them.

    While it’s easy to scroll through subscriptions on YouTube’s mobile interface, accessing subscriptions on desktop is less intuitive and more cumbersome to scroll through (i.e., a card taking up the entire horizontal space, rather than alike to the home page where it’s in a grid). To address this problem, I created a separate entry point to subscriptions on the home page (horizontal scroll) and separated it from the discover using negative space.
